## 1. Value Prop

**For individual AEs/SDRs who self-source their own outbound (initial buyer), and later for sales leaders buying it for their team (v2 expansion): get 80–90% of your prospecting time back — the tool eliminates list-building so your time goes to calls and emails, not research.**

Notes on how this was decided:
- Promise is capacity, not outcome: "you get your time back," not "you'll book more meetings." The latter is a claim the PRD's success metrics don't measure (booking rate depends on the rep's calling/emailing skill, not the tool), so it's deliberately excluded.
- Buyer sequencing: v1 targets the individual rep (self-serve, bottom-up) because that's what's actually buildable given the PRD's v1 scope (Cameron + one provisioned SDR, no general multi-tenant accounts). Selling to sales leaders/VPs for team-wide adoption is a v2 go-to-market expansion, not part of v1's build.

## 2. Business Model

**Hybrid pricing: flat monthly subscription per seat with an included qualified-lead quota, plus metered overage beyond that.** Directional placeholder range: $49–$99/month including ~50–100 fully-qualified leads, then a per-lead charge above that.

Why this model over the alternatives:
- **Pure flat subscription** is simplest to pitch, but risky — cost scales directly with usage (every qualified lead consumes a data-provider credit), so a power user could cost more than they pay. Can't responsibly price this until per-lead data cost is known.
- **Pure usage-based pricing** aligns price to cost cleanly, but fights the product's own value prop — it makes reps ration leads to save money, recreating the scarcity mindset the tool is meant to eliminate.
- **The hybrid** gives predictable base revenue, protects margin on heavy users via overage, and matches category norms (Apollo, Seamless.ai price similarly: seat + credit cap + overage).

**Open dependency:** the specific quota size and overage rate are placeholders, not calculated numbers. Real pricing requires knowing the actual cost per qualified lead from whichever data provider is selected — still an unresolved item in the PRD (see PRD.md, Section 5).
