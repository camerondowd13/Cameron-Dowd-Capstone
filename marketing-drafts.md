## 1. Positioning (Brand Strategist)

**Is the headline clear to a stranger?**
No. "We find them. You close them." has a dangling pronoun — "them" doesn't resolve without also reading the small eyebrow tag above it and the subhead below it. That's two extra reads for one idea. A true 3-second headline should work if someone screenshots just the `<h1>` and shows it to a friend.

**The one thing you're not saying:**
Your actual differentiator is a strict, falsifiable mechanism — a contact only appears if *both* phone and email are verified, no partial credit, and a company only appears if it has at least one contact that clears the full bar. Right now that's softened into "No dead ends," buried as the second of three benefit bullets, with no mechanism explained.

The secondary miss: founder credibility. You're a working AE who lived this exact pain — a strong trust signal for a skeptical AE/SDR audience burned by data-vendor pitches before. Currently this appears nowhere but the footer copyright line.

**How you're different from the obvious alternatives:**
- **ZoomInfo / Apollo / Lusha / Seamless.ai** — contact databases. They hand you rows of data; you still filter, cross-check, and validate it yourself. Sonar's edge isn't better data — it's that it does the filtering and validation work itself and only shows you what already cleared the bar.
- **6sense** — intent/trigger detection only. Tells you a company is "in market" but doesn't resolve a reachable person there. Sonar chains trigger detection *into* contact resolution in one pass.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — manual, one-account-at-a-time, no verified phone/email, and scraping it violates ToS.
- **Clay** — the closest real competitor architecturally (enrichment + waterfall workflows), but requires you or an ops person to build and maintain the workflow. Sonar's pitch is zero setup, zero workflow-building.

The honest positioning: Sonar isn't a better data source, it's an orchestration layer that chains trigger detection → ICP match → contact discovery → dual-channel verification → CRM write-back into one output. That's a workflow claim, not a data-quality claim, and current copy doesn't say this anywhere.

**Headline + subhead rewrites:**

- **Version A — pain-led:**
  > Stop researching. Start dialing.
  > Sonar turns your ICP into a call-ready list — every company qualified, every contact holding a verified phone *and* email. No more bouncing between ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, and Google to build a list you'll use once.

- **Version B — proof-led:**
  > Every contact on your list can actually be reached.
  > Built by an AE who was sick of dead numbers and bounced emails. Sonar chains trigger detection, ICP matching, and phone-and-email verification into one pass — if we can't confirm both, the contact never makes your list.

**B is sharper.** It leads with the one falsifiable, differentiated claim (strict dual-verification, no partial credit) and earns trust through founder credibility in the same breath, instead of restating a generic time-saved pitch any competitor could copy.

## 3. Social Content

**Problem-focused #1**

Before I make a single call, I spend 1-2 hours a day bouncing between ZoomInfo, LinkedIn, Apollo, and Google just to build a list of people worth calling. Some days I find 15 qualified accounts an hour. Other days, 2. The variance alone makes it impossible to plan a day around outreach. That's the actual problem — not that list-building exists, but that it eats the time that should go to revenue-generating work.

**Problem-focused #2**

The worst part of prospecting isn't finding a company — it's finding a person at that company you can actually reach. I can't count how many hours I've burned tracking down a "decision-maker" only to discover there's no working phone number or email attached to them. That contact is dead weight. It looked like progress, but it wasn't. If a name doesn't come with a way to reach them, it shouldn't count as a lead at all.

**Solution-focused #1**

So I'm building Sonar. You give it your ICP — territory, company size, industry — and it hands you back companies that fit, each with a decision-maker who has a verified phone number and email. No browser tabs, no manual lookups, no "let me just double check this contact is real." If a company doesn't have a fully reachable contact, it doesn't show up on your list at all. Just people you can actually call.

**Solution-focused #2**

Most prospecting tools optimize for bigger lists. Sonar does the opposite. The rule is simple: five fields, verified, no partial credit — state, industry, title, phone, email. All five or the contact doesn't make the list. It also writes straight back to HubSpot without creating duplicates or overwriting fields that are already filled in. Smaller list, but every name on it is one you can actually call.

**Personal**

I'm an AE at Stampli. Most weeks I spend 5-10 hours hunting for companies and contacts before I can start actual selling — time that should go to calls, not tab-hopping through ZoomInfo and LinkedIn. I asked around; other AEs and SDRs who self-source their lists have the same problem. So I'm building the tool I wish I had: Sonar. Solo build, still early, but it's solving a problem I live every single week.

- **#7** "find a CFO's direct phone number/email" — "CFO" only appears inside a decorative mock browser result; no real copy targets finding specific titles.

**New copy drafts:**

*Targeting "find a CFO's direct phone number and email":*
Need a CFO's direct line, not a generic switchboard number? Sonar surfaces the exact decision-maker for your ICP — CEO, CFO, CTO, AP Manager, or Director of Finance — and only includes them if both a verified phone number and a verified email are on file. No LinkedIn guesswork, no calling the front desk and getting transferred five times. If we can't confirm a working number and inbox for that person, they don't make your list, so every CFO you dial is one you can actually reach.

*Targeting "auto sync new leads to HubSpot without duplicates":*
Every company and contact Sonar finds gets written straight into HubSpot — no CSV exports, no copy-pasting between tabs. If the company's already in HubSpot, we add the new contact to it; if the contact already exists, we never create a duplicate or overwrite a field you've already filled in. New leads only fill in the blanks. It's built to keep your CRM clean automatically, not to replace it.

*Targeting "identify companies with a buying trigger for outbound sales":*
Sonar starts every search by looking for an active buying trigger — a signal that a company is in-market right now — before falling back to straight ICP-fit industry search when no trigger exists. That means your list leads with the accounts most likely to respond, not just the ones that technically match your firmographics. You skip the manual trigger-hunting across news alerts, funding databases, and job postings, because we've already done it before your list lands.

*Targeting "best prospecting tool for AEs without an SDR team":*
If you're an AE or BDR who has to build your own outbound list — no SDR handing you pre-qualified accounts — Sonar was built for exactly that gap. You already know your ICP; we do the account research, contact discovery, and phone/email verification that would otherwise eat your whole morning. That leaves your time for the one thing that actually moves pipeline: getting on the phone.

*Targeting "build an outbound list that matches your ICP":*
Tell us your territory, company size, industry, and target titles, and Sonar matches against them the same strict way every time — binary fit, no confidence scores, no partial credit. A company only shows up on your list if it has at least one contact that fully clears both the ICP bar and the contact-validity bar. That means the list you get back isn't a starting point for more research — it's a finished, dial-ready list.
